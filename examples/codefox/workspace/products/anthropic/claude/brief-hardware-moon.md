# Claude Hardware Moon Campaign Brief

Create a revised promotional visual for Claude by Anthropic after the prior version was rejected for looking like plastic UI overlays instead of real technological objects.

The new direction should express technological power through physical aerospace hardware, not screen interfaces. The image should feel like a premium cinematic space-industry campaign poster: heavy machines, rockets, landers, metal, ceramic heat shielding, precision structures, propulsion, antennas, solar arrays, and rover instrumentation.

Audience:

- Builders, researchers, operators, and knowledge workers solving difficult technical problems.
- Viewers should read "Claude helps reach the frontier" through the objects in the scene, not through decorative dashboards.

Design direction:

- Primary scene: Claude as a lunar frontier campaign anchored by real space hardware.
- Hero objects: a vertical lunar landing rocket or ascent vehicle, a compact lunar lander, folded landing legs, thruster bells, solar-panel wings, antenna mast, rover, mechanical arm, instrument packages, cable umbilicals, and engineered truss structures.
- The Claude flag can remain, but it should not be the only technological object. It should sit among credible aerospace equipment.
- Make the hardware physically believable: brushed titanium, graphite panels, ceramic white shells, black thermal blankets, orange/clay accent markings, blue-white engine or sensor light, dust on metal edges.
- Avoid plastic UI panels, HUD overlays, floating dashboards, sci-fi holograms, generic cyberpunk neon, and decorative data widgets.
- Keep exact visible copy crisp and sparse.

Suggested visible copy:

- "Claude"
- "抵达更难的问题"
- "For problem solvers reaching further"
