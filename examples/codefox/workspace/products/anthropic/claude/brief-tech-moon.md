# Claude Tech Moon Campaign Brief

Create a revised promotional visual for Claude by Anthropic after the first moon poster was rejected for feeling insufficiently technological.

The new direction should feel like an aerospace mission-control artifact rather than a warm editorial poster. Keep Claude restrained and intelligent, but move the visual language toward precision instruments, orbital telemetry, computational mapping, optical scanning, and command-interface density.

Audience:

- Builders, researchers, operators, and knowledge workers solving hard technical problems.
- Viewers should read the image as "Claude helps push the frontier" within seconds.

Design direction:

- Primary scene: a moon mission poster with a Claude flag as the hero object.
- Signature element: transparent mission-control overlays and lunar scan geometry that wrap around the flag and terrain.
- More technology than the previous version: lidar grid, telemetry arcs, rover/satellite cues, optical sensors, data readouts, and crisp instrument-panel typography.
- Keep the feeling premium and credible, not gaming, anime, cyberpunk, or generic glowing AI.
- Use dark space, moon dust, Claude clay, blue/cyan signal light, and precise high-contrast text.
- Avoid cozy desk objects, paper notebooks, hand-drawn diagrams, warm scrapbook editorial framing, and low-tech workbench props.

Suggested visible copy:

- "Claude"
- "抵达更难的问题"
- "For problem solvers reaching further"
